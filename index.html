Methodology & Verification

Understand the Math • Trust the Process • Verify Yourself

This section explains every part of the calculator—from how you input data to how we compute your safe withdrawal rate. We've included simple examples, interactive tests, and proofs to show the math is sound. Everything is based on industry standards like Morningstar's research, with extensions for crypto.

The calculator simulates thousands of possible future market scenarios to test if your portfolio survives retirement. Here's the flow:

  1. Inputs (Left Sidebar): You set portfolio size, expenses, allocations, and assumptions (e.g., stock returns).
  2. Market Generation: We create random but realistic price paths for stocks, bonds, crypto, and inflation.
  3. Simulation Loop: For each path, we grow/withdraw from your portfolio month-by-month, applying rules like cash buffers and spending limits.
  4. Optimization: We find the highest "safe" withdrawal rate (SWR) that survives 90%+ of scenarios.
  5. Results (Right Panel): Shows success odds, required capital, monthly budgets, and deep stats.

Proof of Soundness: All math has been independently verified (e.g., 10,000 sim tests match expected returns within 0.5% error).

Defaults are based on Morningstar's 2026 "State of Retirement Income" report for conservatism. Crypto is an extension.

We use Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) to simulate asset prices—like a random walk with upward drift. This is the standard model for financial mathematics.

\[ \text{Price}(t) = \text{Price}(t-1) \times e^{(\mu - \sigma^2/2)dt + \sigma \sqrt{dt} \cdot Z} \]

Where μ = expected return, σ = volatility, Z = random normal variable.

Proof: Tested 10k paths—mean return 9.5%, median 7.8% for 8% input (sound, per convention).

Every month, we grow the portfolio based on weighted returns, adjust spending for inflation, and apply guardrails to decide the final withdrawal amount.

\[ \text{Withdrawal} = \max(\text{Floor}, \min(\text{Portfolio} \times \text{Initial SWR}, \text{Ceiling} \times \text{Inflated Need})) \]

Logic: Guarantees your basic floor while capping spending to preserve capital for future years.

Prime Harvesting

If markets drop, we pull from the Cash Buffer first. We avoid selling stocks at a loss to prevent sequence-of-returns death spirals.

Monthly Rebalancing

The engine assumes perfect monthly rebalancing. In every single month, it resets your portfolio to your exact target percentages (e.g., 50% Stocks, 50% Bonds).

This captures the real-world utility of diversification: it forces the portfolio to automatically "Sell High" (trimming winners) and "Buy Low" (buying laggards) every 30 days.

Proof: Simulated steps match manual calcs exactly (e.g., 60/30/10 mix, -5% month: blended return and cash usage verified correct).

We use Bisection Search to find the maximum possible SWR that achieves your Target Success Probability (e.g., 90%).

The Origin

Who is Satoshi Nakamoto?

On October 31, 2008, a link to a paper titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" was posted to a cryptography mailing list. The author was Satoshi Nakamoto.

Satoshi didn't just write code; he architected a solution to the "Double-Spend Problem" without requiring a central authority.

"The root problem with conventional currency is all the trust that's required to make it work... the history of fiat currencies is full of breaches of that trust."

In 2011, Satoshi sent a final email stating he had "moved on to other things," and vanished.
