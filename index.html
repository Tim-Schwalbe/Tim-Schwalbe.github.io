The Mathematics

01. Asset Returns (GBM)

We model asset prices using Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM), the standard model for financial mathematics. Asset prices evolve based on two components:

  • Drift (μ): The expected average return (CAGR).
  • Shock (σZ): The random volatility, where Z is a random number from a Normal Distribution.
Price(t) = Price(t-1) * e^((μ - σ²/2)dt + σ√dt * Z)

02. Correlation (Cholesky)

Assets don't move in isolation. When Stocks crash, Crypto often crashes too. We use Cholesky Decomposition to link the random "dice rolls" of different assets based on your correlation input.

This preserves the structural risk of diversification failing during panics.

The Engine Explained

1. The Solver (The Brain)

How do we know 3.9% is "Safe"? We don't guess. We use a Bisection Search Algorithm.

  • > TARGET: 90% Success Rate
  • > TRY: 4.0% Withdrawal... Result: 88% (FAIL) -> Too High
  • > TRY: 3.5% Withdrawal... Result: 95% (PASS) -> Too Low
  • > TRY: 3.75% Withdrawal... Result: 92% (PASS) -> Close...
  • > ... (Repeats 25 times for extreme precision) ...
  • > FOUND: 3.92% is the Maximum Safe Rate.

2. The Simulation Loop (The Heart)

For every single month of the 30-year horizon, the engine performs these 4 distinct steps for each of the 3,000 simulations.

Step 01
The Market Pulse

We roll the dice. Using the Correlation Matrix, we generate linked returns for Stocks, Bonds, and Crypto. If the market crashes, all assets might drop together.

Step 02
Inflation Check

We adjust your "Target Spending" based on this month's specific inflation. If inflation spiked, your requested monthly cash needs go UP to maintain lifestyle.

Step 03
The Decision (Dynamic Spending)

The engine decides how much you actually withdraw:

Withdrawal = Max(Floor, Min(Upside, Ceiling))

It guarantees your Floor (Basic Needs) but CAPS your Upside (Luxury) to prevent overspending.

Step 04
The Shield (Cash Buffer)

Critical Logic: If the portfolio dropped this month (Return < 0), we use "Prime Harvesting" . We withdraw from the Cash Buffer first. We do NOT sell Stocks when they are down. This prevents "Sequence of Returns Risk" death spirals.

The Origin

Who is Satoshi Nakamoto?

On October 31, 2008, a link to a paper titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" was posted to a cryptography mailing list. The author was Satoshi Nakamoto.

Satoshi didn't just write code; he architected a solution to the "Double-Spend Problem" without requiring a central authority.

"The root problem with conventional currency is all the trust that's required to make it work... the history of fiat currencies is full of breaches of that trust."

In 2011, Satoshi sent a final email stating he had "moved on to other things," and vanished.
